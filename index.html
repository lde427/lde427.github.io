Dongeon Lee

KAIST, Daejeon, Republic of Korea

I am an Integrated PhD student at Cho Chun Shik Graduate School of Mobility, KAIST, where I work under the guidance of Prof. Namwoo Kang at Smart Design Lab. My research focuses on generative design, data-driven design, and surrogate model-based design optimization, with a particular emphasis on uncertainty quantification. I am deeply interested in applying artificial intelligence (AI) to engineering challenges, especially in virtual product/mobility development.

Before joining KAIST, I worked as an Associate Research Engineer at Hanwha Defense (Currently Hanwha Aerospace), where I contributed to integrated logistics support (ILS) for defense systems like infantry fighting vehicle (IFV). I also hold a B.S. in Mechanical Engineering from Pusan National University (PNU), where I graduated summa cum laude.

Beyond my academic and professional pursuits, I am passionate about bridging AI and real-world physical systems, with a focus on multi-scale and multi-physics considerations in virtual product/mobility development. You can explore my latest research and projects here.
